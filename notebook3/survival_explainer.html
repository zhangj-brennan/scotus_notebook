Survival Rate Explainer

A sequence of charts using Supreme Court justices to explain how survival rate works. Each chart starts from the same base scatterplot: start date on the x-axis, and years in office on the y-axis.

Scene 1

A scatterplot of all justices

Here are all the justices. The y-axis shows their years in office, and the x-axis shows the date they started.

Scene 2

A horizontal line at 10 years

What is their survival rate at 10 years? Out of all justices, how many stayed in office at least 10 years?

Scene 3

What about tenure at 20 years?

Now the threshold moves to 20 years. Out of all justices, how many were still in office at least that long?

Scene 4

An interactive survival threshold

Here you can drag the line up and down to see how survival rate works at each length of tenure.

Drag the red dashed line vertically.
Scene 5

How has that survival rate changed over time?

Add a vertical line at 1950 and keep the horizontal line at 20 years. The large percentages show the share in each side of the split that falls into each quadrant.

Scene 6

Interactive time split and tenure threshold

Now both lines are draggable. Move the vertical line left and right for time, and the horizontal line up and down for tenure. The percentages update automatically.

Drag the red horizontal line and the blue vertical line.
