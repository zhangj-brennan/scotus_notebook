Supreme court tenure over time

The Supreme Court has increasingly been subject to calls for reform, including imposing term limits on the justices. Term limits would ensure that no one person holds too much public power for too long and allow for regular turnover on the bench; this would invite more diverse perspectives that reflect the evolving views and composition of the electorate to make the country’s most important legal decisions.

Capping tenure at, say, 18 years would also help correct the emerging trend of excessive terms. Justices who were appointed to the bench during the first 180 years of the Court’s history served for a median of 15 years. But starting in the 1970s, median tenure increased to 27.5 years. These lengthening terms mean justices can spend decades in an elite and unaccountable bubble, where they can leave a substantially greater imprint on the Court and the country than their predecessors.
