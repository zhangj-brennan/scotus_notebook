A survival rate shows the chance that something continues past a certain point in time. In this chart, it represents the probability that a Supreme Court justice is still serving after a given number of years on the Court.

For example, if the survival rate at 10 years is 0.80, that means about 80% of justices who started are still on the Court after 10 years, while about 20% have left. As time goes on, the survival line gradually drops because more justices retire, die, or otherwise leave the Court.
