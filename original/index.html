Over their term, a justice can be in many different cohorts. Each cohort is a unique set of justices serving at the same time.

There are 222 cohorts total.

Jackson is only in 1 cohort because she is the newest Justice and has 8 peers.

Some longer serving justices are part of many cohorts, Brennan served in 14 different cohorts, with 22 peers.

Note court sizes varied over time until recently.

A cohort is only created when a new person is confirmed, to compensate for the gap between when someone leaves and new justice is confirmed.
