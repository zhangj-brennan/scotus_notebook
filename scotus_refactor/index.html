Notes on Supreme Court Tenure and Age
There have been 116 justices on the supreme court.
That is not a lot of data points.
It can be hard to see how the length of tenure has changed over time.
When grouped by decade we can see peaks in median tenure length in 1820 and from 1970 to now.
Grouped into 30 or more year intervals, the change is less noisy.

It is not out of the ordinary for justices to die on the bench.
44% of Justices have died on the bench. 35% retire.
Retirement doesn't mean a short career.
Retired justices have a median tenure of 19.7 years. Longer than the group as a whole(16.3) and longer than those who died(16.2) on the bench.
Death may cut off otherwise long careers.

Current justices
Over their term, a justice can be in many different cohorts. Each cohort is a unique set of justices serving at the same time.

There are 222 cohorts total.

Jackson is only in 1 cohort because she is the newest Justice and has 8 peers.

Some longer serving justices are part of many cohorts, Brennan served in 14 different cohorts, with 22 peers.

Note court sizes varied over time until recently.

A cohort is only created when a new person is confirmed, to compensate for the gap between when someone leaves and new justice is confirmed.
