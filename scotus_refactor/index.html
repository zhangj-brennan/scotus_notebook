Notes on Supreme Court Tenure and Age
There have been 116 justices on the supreme court.
Here are some ways of understanding how long they serve.

It can be hard to see how tenure has changed over time when each decade only yields a few justices.

When grouped by decade we can see peaks in median tenure length in 1820 and from 1970 to now.
Grouped into 20+ year intervals, the trend is less noisy.

He is ranked 5th, and will be 2nd in just over 2 months.

If sorted by most recent, out of the 12 justices(dark grey) whose tenure lasted past 1990 have served longer terms, only 1, Souter was on the bench for less than 20 years.
It is not out of the ordinary for justices to die on the bench.
44% of Justices have died on the bench. 35% retire.
Retirement doesn't mean a short career.
Retired justices have a median tenure of 19.7 years. Longer than the group as a whole(16.3) and longer than those who died(16.2) on the bench.
Death cut off otherwise long careers.

*Age is calculated using Jan. 1st of birth year for now.

Over their term, a justice can work with any number of justices depending on how fast the court turns over during their tenure.

Some serve with as few as 6 other justices - in the early years of the court when size varied.

*Exception need to fix - Jackson was sworn in before Breyer finished his term, so she is shown to overlap him.
Less overlaps have occured in the past 50 years as term lengths are longer.
A network can be formed using how much a justice overlaps in years.
Each justice is a node, and there is a link between everyone they served with.
The strength/length of the links is determined by the length of time they overlap.


This in general is less relevant to exploring tenure. Central figures who are well connected are the alrger nodes in the center. Other network measures like components were implemented but not helpful.
The number of new justices per decade shows that turnover has been low in the past 30 years.
Turnover last peaked in 1940.
Justices have a higher survival rate post 1950 than pre.

Survival rate is calculated as the % of justices that continue beyond each year of tenure. For example, 100% of justices made it past the first year. And the rate falls as tenure grows when Justices leave the court.
Hazard - how likely are justices to leave at each point in their tenure? This is very noisy because of bin size.
